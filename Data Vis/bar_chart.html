Welcome to this interactive data visualization representing internally displaced people (IDPs) across the provinces of Afghanistan. At its heart, you'll find a heat map shaped like the country itself. Each province's color is directly related to the number of IDPs - the darker the color, the higher the count.

This visualization allows you to switch between two distinct views: 'Arrived IDPs' and 'Fled IDPs.' 'Arrived IDPs' shows you how many displaced people have moved into each province, while 'Fled IDPs' highlights how many have left. To find the exact number of IDPs in a particular area, simply hover your mouse over the province.

For a quick understanding of what each color represents, look to the legend located beneath the heat map. Here, color shades are linked with specific IDP values.

Your exploration doesn't stop at the heat map. By clicking on a province, you'll activate a bar chart on the right side of your screen. This chart provides a visual comparison of the number of IDPs who have arrived and those who have fled the selected province.

To deepen your understanding, the bar chart includes a drop-down box. This allows you to compare IDP data from different years, providing a historical perspective on displacement trends within the selected province. Enjoy your exploration and analysis of this complex issue with our interactive visualization tool.

Arrivals vs. Fled IDPs by Province

Below the initial visualizations, you'll find a second, equally interactive heatmap. This one is structured in a grid, with each tile representing a specific month from a given year between 2017 and 2023. Each tile is color-coded to represent the number of fatalities during that time period - the darker the shade of red, the more conflict events have occurred.

As you move your mouse over a tile, a box to the right of the visualization will display detailed information for that month. It will include the specific date, the total number of conflict events, and the number of fatalities associated with these conflicts.

This second heatmap is not an isolated piece of data. It's intended to be viewed in conjunction with the IDP (Internally Displaced People) data shown above. Though it's important to note, while these two sets of data can be correlated, correlation does not imply causation. In other words, while trends might appear to align between conflict events and the numbers of arrived or fled IDPs, this does not necessarily mean that conflict events directly cause these displacement patterns. The relationship between these variables is complex, and multiple factors are likely at play.

By analyzing both sets of data concurrently, you're provided with a richer, more nuanced understanding of displacement in Afghanistan. This tool, therefore, serves as a powerful instrument to delve into the intricate dynamics between conflict events and internal displacement within the nation.
